# Benralizumab for hypereosinophilic syndrome

## E156 Micro-Paper Draft

### Question

In patients with FIP1L1::PDGFRA-negative hypereosinophilic syndrome, does benralizumab add-on therapy reduce HES flares compared with placebo plus background therapy?

### Evidence

The primary release evidence is the 2026 NATRON phase 3 randomized trial (`NCT04191304`), which randomized 133 patients and reported a lower time-to-first-HES-flare hazard with benralizumab. The extracted primary effect is HR 0.35 (95% CI 0.18 to 0.69).

AACT also identifies the earlier randomized HESIL5R study (`NCT02130882`). Its registry-posted randomized endpoint is a biomarker response: 50% reduction in peripheral blood eosinophilia at 12 weeks, 9/10 with benralizumab versus 3/10 with placebo. The derived RR is 3.00 (95% CI 1.14 to 7.91). This endpoint is retained as supportive evidence and is not pooled with the NATRON time-to-flare HR.

### Methods

Trial identifiers and registry metadata were verified from the local AACT flat-file snapshot exported on 2026-06-04. The project searches by intervention name (`benralizumab` / `Fasenra`) rather than class. AACT intervention types are lowercased before filtering, and the extraction scripts fail closed if required tables, columns, NCT IDs, or HESIL5R source counts are missing.

The dashboard computes log-scale fixed-effect and DerSimonian-Laird random-effects estimates from loaded comparable effects. Because only one eligible HR is available for the primary endpoint, the dashboard reports a single-study effect preview rather than a multi-study pooled estimate.

### Result

Benralizumab reduced the hazard of first HES flare in NATRON: HR 0.35 (95% CI 0.18 to 0.69). This is new phase 3 evidence published in 2026. The prior randomized HESIL5R trial supports eosinophil depletion but does not provide a comparable clinical time-to-flare HR for pooling.

### Interpretation

The current shippable claim is narrow: NATRON provides source-backed phase 3 randomized evidence that benralizumab reduces HES flare risk versus placebo plus background therapy in FIP1L1::PDGFRA-negative HES. This project should not claim a pooled clinical meta-analysis until at least one additional comparable randomized time-to-flare effect is identified.
